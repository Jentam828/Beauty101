Plant Based Diet

植物性飲食指南

For the woman in her mid-50s who is done being told what her body can't do — and ready to hear what it still can, with the right food on her plate.

Food· 9 min read · 九分鐘· Entry No. 003

🌿 🌿 🌿

There is a particular kind of invisibility that descends on women in their mid-50s. The medical system starts talking past them. The wellness industry talks down to them. And the food advice — when it exists at all — tends to be either catastrophically complicated or aggressively cheerful in a way that feels faintly insulting. This post is neither. It is just food. Real food. And what it can quietly, reliably do for a body navigating one of its more demanding chapters.

更年期後的女性，常常被忽視。
醫療系統說不完的風險，健康產業賣不完的補品。
這篇文章只談食物。真正的食物。以及它能做到的事。

"Eat food. Not too much. Mostly plants."
吃真正的食物。不要吃太多。以植物為主。 — Michael Pollan, In Defense of Food · 《為食物辯護》

Seven words. Michael Pollan distilled decades of nutrition research into seven words and the nutrition industry has never quite forgiven him for it. Because he was right, and being right so simply puts a lot of people out of business. The science behind plant-based eating for post-menopausal women is extensive, peer-reviewed, and unglamorous in the best possible way. No superfoods. No subscription boxes. Just plants, prepared simply, eaten consistently.

Three things need the most attention at this stage: bones, muscles, and the mind. Conveniently, plants have something useful to say about all three.

🦴
Pillar One · 第一支柱
Strengthening Your Bones
強化骨骼

Estrogen kept your bones dense. When it left, it took some of that protection with it. Bone resorption — the process where old bone breaks down — now happens faster than new bone can be built. This is not a flaw. It is biology. But biology responds to food.

Post-menopausal women need around 1,200mg of calcium daily — significantly more than before menopause. Combined with vitamin D (800–2,000 IU daily), this reliably slows bone loss and reduces fracture risk, according to 2025 evidence-based guidelines from Frontiers in Nutrition.

The good news: you do not need dairy to hit those numbers. Dark leafy greens — kale, bok choy, collard greens — deliver calcium in a form the body absorbs well. Tofu set with calcium sulphate is exceptionally rich. Fortified plant milks, white beans, tahini, almonds. Varied, accessible, not expensive.

Vitamin D is trickier on a plant-based diet. Mushrooms exposed to sunlight produce some. Fortified foods help. But a supplement is often the most reliable option — check with your doctor before adding one.

Miso Soup with Tofu, Bok Choy & Sesame
豆腐、小白菜、芝麻味噌湯

Warm water or dashi base. A tablespoon of white miso. Silken tofu cubed. A handful of bok choy wilted in the broth. Sesame seeds on top. Done in ten minutes. Calcium from the tofu, bok choy, and sesame. Magnesium from the miso. Warming and not complicated.

Why it works: Tofu set with calcium sulphate provides more calcium per serving than a glass of cow's milk. Bok choy has a higher calcium absorption rate than dairy. The sesame seeds aren't just decoration.
💪
Pillar Two · 第二支柱
Supporting Your Muscles
支撐肌肉

Muscle loss after menopause is real, gradual, and largely underreported. The medical term is sarcopenia. The practical translation is: the less muscle you carry, the slower your metabolism, the harder the weight becomes to manage, and the more fragile your body gets over time. None of this is inevitable. Protein and movement are the two most reliable tools you have.

Protein requirements actually increase after menopause — to 1.1–1.5g per kilogram of body weight daily — to preserve lean muscle mass. Plant-based proteins from tempeh, lentils, chickpeas, and quinoa are fully adequate when varied. A 2025 study found a vegan diet did not compromise muscle protein synthesis in adults compared to omnivorous diets, provided total protein intake was sufficient.

The plant-based world has a protein problem — not a real one, but a perception one. People assume plants can't deliver. They can. The key is variety and volume. Lentils. Edamame. Chickpeas. Tempeh. Black beans. Quinoa — one of the few complete plant proteins, meaning it contains all nine essential amino acids. Eat enough of these daily and your muscles have what they need.

One thing worth knowing: we absorb protein slightly less efficiently as we age. This means the serving sizes that worked at 35 may need to go up a little at 55. Not dramatically. Just a bit more intentional.

Lemon Herb Lentil Bowl with Roasted Vegetables
檸檬香草扁豆碗佐烤蔬菜

Cook one cup green or French lentils until just tender. Roast whatever vegetables are in the fridge — broccoli, sweet potato, red onion — at high heat until caramelised. Dress with lemon juice, olive oil, cumin, and fresh parsley. Top with a spoonful of tahini thinned with water. Pumpkin seeds for crunch.

Why it works: Lentils deliver around 18g of protein per cooked cup, plus iron and magnesium. Pumpkin seeds add zinc, which supports muscle repair. The whole bowl takes about 30 minutes and tastes better the next day.

Pillar Three · 第三支柱
Supporting Your Mental Health
支持心理健康

This one deserves its own conversation. Mood shifts during and after menopause are not imaginary, not weakness, and not permanent. They are the result of estrogen's departure from a nervous system that relied on it for decades. Anxiety, low mood, disrupted sleep, brain fog — these are real, common, and often under-treated. Food is not a cure. But the gut-brain connection is well-documented, and what you eat directly affects the serotonin your body produces. Up to 90% of serotonin is made in the gut, not the brain. Feed the gut well and the brain notices.

Research cited in the American Journal of Medicine found that diets high in ultra-processed foods are associated with nearly 50% higher risk of depression, while every additional 100g of fruits or vegetables consumed daily is linked to a 3% lower risk of depression. Magnesium — abundant in plant foods — plays a direct role in mood regulation, sleep quality, and stress response.

Magnesium is the undersung hero of plant-based eating. Dark chocolate, spinach, pumpkin seeds, black beans, avocado, whole grains — all rich in magnesium, which the body converts into GABA, a neurotransmitter that helps the brain slow down and rest. This is why a magnesium-rich dinner often means a better night's sleep. And sleep, at this stage of life, is not a luxury. It is infrastructure.

Omega-3 fatty acids are also worth mentioning — linked to cognitive resilience and lower inflammation. On a plant-based diet, the best sources are flaxseeds, chia seeds, walnuts, and hemp seeds. An algae-based DHA/EPA supplement is worth discussing with your doctor if oily fish is not in your diet.

Walnut & Dark Chocolate Overnight Oats with Chia
核桃黑巧克力奇亞籽隔夜燕麥

The night before: half a cup of rolled oats, one tablespoon chia seeds, one cup oat or almond milk, a teaspoon of maple syrup. Stir and refrigerate. In the morning: top with a small handful of walnuts, a square of dark chocolate (70% or above) broken into pieces, and whatever fruit is around. Preparation time: four minutes.

Why it works: Oats stabilise blood sugar, which directly affects mood. Chia seeds deliver omega-3s and magnesium. Walnuts are one of the richest plant sources of ALA omega-3. The dark chocolate is not optional — it contains magnesium and makes everything better.

A Few Things Worth Saying Plainly

None of this requires becoming a different person or overhauling your entire kitchen. Pollan's genius was in the simplicity of his instruction. Eat food — real food, not products engineered in laboratories. Not too much — portions matter, but so does enjoyment. Mostly plants — not exclusively, not fanatically, but as the foundation.

Start with one meal. The lentil bowl. The miso soup. The overnight oats. Make it three times. By the third time it won't feel like an effort — it will just feel like dinner. That's the whole strategy. There isn't a more complicated one worth following.

不需要變成另一個人。
不需要重新整理廚房。
從一道菜開始。做三次。
第三次的時候，它就只是晚餐了。

Post-menopause is not a decline. It is a recalibration — one that rewards attention and punishes neglect, like most things worth keeping. Your bones are still building. Your muscles still respond to what you eat and how you move. Your gut is still listening. The food on your plate is still, quietly and without drama, doing its work.

You've navigated harder transitions than this one. Feed yourself accordingly.

更年期後不是衰退。是重新校準。
你的骨骼還在重建。你的肌肉還在回應。
你的腸道還在聆聽你給它的食物。
好好吃飯。你值得。
