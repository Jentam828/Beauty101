Nikko, Japan

日本 · 日光

Shrines, waterfalls, marshlands, and a capsule hotel. Ten days in Japan, starting with one night inside a pod at Narita and ending somewhere very quiet in the mountains.

✈ SFO → NRT  ·  Oct 16–26, 2025  ·  10 Days

Travels· 10 min read · 十分鐘· Entry No. 001

⛩️ 🍂 🏔️

Japan has a way of making you feel like you've been doing everything wrong. Not in a judgmental way — the Japanese are far too polite for that — but in the quiet, insistent way that a very well-organised place makes you aware of how disorganised everywhere else is. The trains run on time. The streets are clean. Even the capsule hotel at the airport has a system, and the system works, and you lie in your pod at eleven at night thinking: why is everything else in my life like this?

日本有一種讓你覺得自己一直做錯事的方式。
不是批判你——日本人太有禮貌了，不會那樣做——
而是一種安靜的、持續的方式，讓你意識到其他地方有多混亂。
火車準時。街道乾淨。就連機場的膠囊旅館都有系統，而且系統有效。

背景介紹

A Brief History of Nikko

— ⛩ —

Nikko sits in the mountains of Tochigi Prefecture, about 140 kilometres north of Tokyo. The name means "sunlight" — 日光 — and on a clear autumn day, with the maples turning and the cedar forests glowing, it earns it. The area has been considered sacred since the 8th century, when the Buddhist monk Shōdō Shōnin climbed its peaks and established the first temples there in 766 CE. For centuries it remained a place of religious retreat, relatively inaccessible and intentionally removed from the world.

Everything changed in 1617, when Tokugawa Ieyasu — the founder and first shogun of the Edo period, the man who unified Japan after a century of civil war — was enshrined at Nikko following his death. His grandson, Tokugawa Iemitsu, then spent twenty years and the equivalent of a small country's GDP building the Tōshōgū Shrine complex to honour him. Thousands of craftsmen were brought in. The result is one of the most lavishly decorated religious sites in Japan — part shrine, part mausoleum, entirely overwhelming. Nikko became a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1999.

Beyond the shrines, Nikko is also a gateway to Nikkō National Park — mountains, waterfalls, volcanic lakes, and marshlands that feel completely separate from the ornate grandeur below. Two very different worlds, twenty minutes apart by bus.

日光位於栃木縣山區，距東京約一百四十公里。
自西元766年起，佛教僧侶在此建立寺廟，此地便被視為神聖之地。
1617年，江戶幕府創始人德川家康在此設靈，其孫德川家光耗費二十年建造東照宮。
日光於1999年被列為UNESCO世界遺產。
除了神社，日光也是日光國立公園的入口——瀑布、火山湖、濕原，另一個世界。
第一站

First Stop: Narita Airport

— ✈ —
💊
Capsule Hotel · 膠囊旅館 · Oct 17
9h Nine Hours — Narita Airport
成田機場 9h 膠囊旅館

The 9h Nine Hours capsule hotel sits inside Narita Airport Terminal 1 — which means you clear customs, check in, and are horizontal within about fifteen minutes if you move efficiently, which after a transpacific flight is approximately all you are capable of. The concept is Japanese in its precision: sleep is broken into nine hours, one hour of waking time, and one hour for bathing. Hence the name.

The capsule itself is a moulded fibreglass pod — wider than a coffin, narrower than a studio apartment, which covers the full range of relevant comparisons. There is a privacy curtain, a reading light, a small shelf, and an alarm. The common shower facilities are clean to an almost aggressive degree. If you are claustrophobic, this is not your stop. If you are not — and I am not — it is one of those experiences that sounds worse than it is, costs less than a hotel room, and gives you something to talk about at dinner for the next six months.

Try it at least once. The novelty is real, the sleep is adequate, and there is something genuinely satisfying about a system so perfectly designed for exactly one purpose. Also the shared lounge at 6am, full of jet-lagged strangers in matching grey robes, has the quiet camaraderie of people who have all made the same unusual decision and are quietly pleased about it.
→
Located directly inside Narita Airport Terminal 1 — no transportation needed. Follow signs from arrivals. Check-in is at the hotel desk on the terminal level. 位於成田機場第一航廈內，無需交通。從入境大廳跟著指示牌走即可。
💡
Book in advance — especially during peak travel seasons. Walk-ins are possible but not guaranteed. 建議提前預訂，旺季期間可能客滿。
第二站

Arriving in Nikko

— 🚄 —
🏯
Where I Stayed · 住宿 · Oct 18–20
Okunikko Konishi Hotel
奧日光小西旅館

The Okunikko Konishi is a traditional Japanese inn — a ryokan in character if not always in name — tucked into the quieter, higher reaches of Okunikko, the inner mountain area beyond the shrines. Charming is the right word. Outdated is also the right word. Both are true simultaneously, which is a combination the Japanese do with more grace than most.

The grounds are peaceful in the specific way that places away from everything are peaceful — not because something nice is happening, but because nothing much is happening at all, and your nervous system, confused, interprets this as luxury. Breakfast and dinner are included and genuinely reasonable — traditional Japanese meals, locally sourced, the kind of food that makes you wonder why you ever eat anything else.

One thing worth knowing before you go: there are no restaurants nearby. I mean none. The kind of none where you look at your phone, look around, look at your phone again, and accept that dinner is happening at the hotel or not at all. Make your meal reservations when you book the room. This is not a suggestion. It is the only option available to you.
1
From Narita Airport, take the Narita Express (N'EX) to Shinjuku Station (~90 min). 成田機場搭乘成田特快（N'EX）至新宿站，約90分鐘。
2
From Shinjuku, take the Tobu Nikko Line Limited Express from Asakusa Station directly to Nikko Station (~2 hrs). Or take the JR Shinkansen to Utsunomiya, then local JR Nikko Line (~45 min). 從淺草站搭乘東武日光線特急至日光站，約2小時。或新幹線至宇都宮，再轉JR日光線。
3
From Nikko Station, take the Tobu Bus bound for Yumoto Onsen. Alight at the stop closest to your hotel (~40–50 min from station). 從日光站搭乘東武巴士往湯元溫泉方向，在最近的站下車，約40–50分鐘。
💡
Get a Nikko All Area Pass — covers unlimited Tobu trains and buses in the region for 2–4 days. Saves money and removes the headache of buying individual tickets. 建議購買「日光全地區通票」，涵蓋東武電車與巴士，2至4日無限搭乘。
Nikko is two places at once — the ornate and the elemental, the human ambition to honour the dead and the indifferent beauty of mountains that were here long before anyone thought to build anything.
日光是兩個地方同時存在——華麗與原始，人類的野心與山川的漠然。
第三站

Senjogahara Marshland

— 🌿 —
🌾
Hike · 健行 · Okunikko
Senjogahara Marshland
戰場ヶ原

The name Senjogahara translates roughly to "battlefield plain" — a reference to an old legend in which the god of Mount Nantai and the god of Mount Akagi fought here for dominion over the land, with the serpent god ultimately flooding the area and creating what is now the marshland. The gods resolved their dispute. The marshland remained. Nobody seems to mind.

Today it is a protected wetland within Nikko National Park — roughly 400 hectares of highland bog, wooden boardwalks, and views of the surrounding mountains that are quietly astonishing. In autumn, the grasses turn gold and rust. The walk is relatively flat and easy, which makes it accessible without making it boring. You will share the trail with serious hikers, elderly Japanese couples, and the occasional child who is having a better time than anyone else.

This is the kind of place that doesn't announce itself. You walk in, the boardwalk stretches out ahead of you, the mountains are there on all sides, and something in your chest quietly unclenches. Bring water, wear layers — the elevation makes it cooler than you expect — and allow more time than the map suggests. You will want to stop more than you planned.
1
From Nikko Station, take the Tobu Bus toward Yumoto Onsen and alight at Akanuma bus stop (~45 min). This is the eastern trailhead of Senjogahara. 從日光站搭東武巴士往湯元溫泉，在「赤沼」站下車，約45分鐘。這是戰場ヶ原東側入口。
2
The main trail runs approximately 4km one way from Akanuma to Yudaki Falls, following the boardwalk through the marshland. A full loop including Yugawa River takes 3–4 hours at a relaxed pace. 主步道從赤沼到湯滑瀑布，單程約4公里。包含湯川河沿岸的完整環形路線約需3至4小時。
💡
The Low Moor Line (低公害バス) — a low-emission shuttle bus — also runs seasonally within the area and can shorten the return journey. Check schedules at Nikko Station tourist information. 季節性低公害巴士可縮短回程時間。請在日光站旅遊資訊處確認時刻表。
第四站

Yudaki Falls, Yugawa River & Kotaki Falls

— 💧 —
🌊
Hike Loop · 環形健行 · Okunikko
Yudaki Falls · Yugawa River · Kotaki Falls
湯滑瀑布 · 湯川 · 小滝

Yudaki Falls is where Lake Yunoko empties — a wide, thundering cascade about 70 metres tall that drops dramatically into the valley below. Unlike the careful, composed aesthetics of the shrine complex, Yudaki is simply loud and large and completely uninterested in making a good impression. It doesn't need to. The Yugawa River then carries that water downstream through a forested gorge, and the trail follows it — past smaller falls, over wooden bridges, through cedar and larch that in October are doing everything they can with the available light.

Kotaki Falls is quieter and smaller than Yudaki — a narrower drop into a dark pool that feels almost hidden. Most visitors walk past it too quickly. Worth stopping for.

The loop connecting all three — Yudaki, the river trail, and Kotaki — is one of the better hikes I've done in Japan. Not because it's dramatic, exactly, but because it changes character every twenty minutes. Waterfall, then river, then forest, then a quieter waterfall, then you're back. The whole loop runs about 3–4 hours. October is the right month for it — the autumn colour here is not subtle.
1
From Nikko Station, take the Tobu Bus to Yutaki bus stop (湯滝) — the stop closest to Yudaki Falls (~55 min from station). 從日光站搭東武巴士到「湯滝」站，約55分鐘，即可抵達湯滑瀑布入口。
2
From Yudaki Falls, follow the Yugawa riverside trail downstream to Kotaki Falls, then continue to Akanuma. From Akanuma, buses return to Nikko Station. 從湯滑瀑布沿湯川步道往下游走，經小滝，再至赤沼。從赤沼搭巴士返回日光站。
💡
This trail connects naturally with Senjogahara Marshland — both can be done in the same day if you start early. A full day is recommended. 此步道與戰場ヶ原相連，早出發可以一天完成兩個景點。建議留一整天。
第五站

Nikkō Tōshōgū Shrine

— ⛩ —
⛩️
UNESCO World Heritage Site · 世界遺產
Nikkō Tōshōgū Shrine
日光東照宮

Tōshōgū was built between 1634 and 1636 under the orders of Tokugawa Iemitsu, to enshrine his grandfather Tokugawa Ieyasu — who had requested a modest burial and got the opposite. The complex contains more than a dozen buildings, each decorated to a degree that the word "ornate" fails to capture. Gold leaf, lacquerwork, intricate wood carvings — the famous Sleeping Cat (Nemuri-Neko) and the Three Wise Monkeys (see no evil, hear no evil, speak no evil) are both here, both smaller than you expect, both surrounded by people taking photographs.

The grounds themselves are set among 13,000 cedar trees, many of them centuries old. In October, the contrast between the extravagant shrine buildings and the quiet, towering cedars around them is striking in a way that photographs don't quite communicate. The scale is the thing. You have to be there.

Allow at least two hours. More if you are the kind of person who reads every information placard — and you should be, because the history here is genuinely interesting and not always what you expect. The greenery alone is worth the trip. It is one of those places that has been photographed so many times that you arrive with expectations, and it still exceeds them.
1
From Nikko Station, take the Tobu Bus bound for Chuzenji Onsen or Yumoto Onsen and alight at Shinkyo bus stop (~10 min, ¥170). Or walk — it's about 30 minutes uphill through cedar-lined streets, which is its own experience. 從日光站搭東武巴士至「神橋」站，約10分鐘。或步行約30分鐘，沿途杉木林立，值得走走。
2
From Shinkyo, it's a 10-minute walk uphill through the shrine approach (sando) to the Tōshōgū entrance. Entry fee is ¥1,300 for adults (confirm current pricing on arrival). 從神橋步行約10分鐘上坡至東照宮入口。成人入場費約1,300日圓（請以現場公告為準）。
💡
Go early. The shrine opens at 8am. Tour groups arrive mid-morning. The difference in crowd size between 8am and 10am is significant. 建議早點去。神社8點開門。旅行團通常10點後大量湧入。早去早享受。
🎫
Nikko All Area Pass — Buy at Asakusa Station or online. Covers unlimited Tobu trains from Tokyo and all buses within the Nikko area for 2 or 4 days. Essential. 「日光全地區通票」——在淺草站或網路購買。涵蓋東武電車與日光地區所有巴士。必買。
🍂
Best time to visit — Mid to late October for autumn foliage. Nikko's autumn colour is among the best in Japan and the reason crowds are heavier in October. Plan accordingly. 最佳時機——十月中至下旬，楓葉季節。這也是人潮最多的時候，請做好心理準備。
🧥
Dress in layers — Okunikko sits at around 1,200m elevation. Even in October it can be genuinely cold, especially on the marshland trails and near the falls. 穿著分層——奧日光海拔約1,200公尺，十月份可能很冷，健行時尤其明顯。
🍽️
Book hotel meals in advance — If you're staying in Okunikko, there are no restaurants in the immediate area. Reserve breakfast and dinner when you book the room. This is non-negotiable. 提前預訂旅館餐點——奧日光附近沒有餐廳。訂房時同步預訂早晚餐，這不是建議，是必須。
🌐
Pocket WiFi or SIM card — Google Maps works in Japan and is reliable for bus routes and trail navigation. Get connected at the airport on arrival. 租借隨身WiFi或購買SIM卡——Google Maps在日本可用，對查詢巴士路線和步道很有幫助。
