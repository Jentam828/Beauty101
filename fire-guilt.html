The Guilt of FIRE

財務自由的罪惡感

On walking away from a well-paying job, the strange weight of freedom, and why "congratulations" never quite covers what you're actually feeling.

Personal Therapy· 8 min read · 八分鐘· Entry No. 001

✦ ✦ ✦

FIRE stands for Financial Independence, Retire Early. It is a movement, a strategy, a Reddit community, and depending on who you ask, either the smartest thing a person can do or a elaborate excuse to stop doing anything useful. I achieved it. I walked away from a well-paying job. And then I sat down, looked at my calendar — completely, gloriously empty — and felt, inexplicably, terrible.

財務自由了。提早退休了。行事曆空白一片。
然後呢？然後我坐在那裡，莫名其妙地感到很糟糕。

Nobody warns you about this part. The FIRE literature is very good at telling you how to save, how to invest, what your withdrawal rate should be, how to calculate your number. It is considerably less helpful on the subject of what happens to your identity when you remove the thing that has been defining it for the past twenty years. Turns out your sense of self was more entangled with your job title than you thought. Who knew. Well — everybody knew. You just didn't think it applied to you.

The Guilt Arrives Uninvited, Like a Relative

The guilt has several flavours. There is the social guilt — the mild panic when someone at a dinner party asks what you do, and you have to explain that you don't, exactly, do anything, and watch their face cycle through confusion, envy, and something that looks uncomfortably like judgement, all within about four seconds. There is the productivity guilt — the creeping suspicion that a person who is not generating output is not a real person. And there is the age guilt, which is perhaps the strangest of all: the feeling that retirement is something that happens at sixty-five, after decades of earning it, and that doing it earlier is somehow — cheating.

罪惡感有好幾種口味。
社交版：「你現在做什麼？」「呃……我不太做什麼。」
生產力版：不產出任何東西的人，算人嗎？
年齡版：退休不是應該六十五歲才輪到我嗎？

Retirement at sixty-five is considered wisdom.
Retirement at forty is considered suspicious.
The math is identical. Only the optics differ.
六十五歲退休叫智慧。四十歲退休叫可疑。數學一樣，只是觀感不同。

The Corporate Job Was Never the Point

Here is what I have come to understand. The corporate job was never the point — it was the vehicle. You don't mourn the bus after you've arrived at the destination. You got on the bus because you needed to get somewhere. You got somewhere. The bus has moved on. This is correct. This is how buses work.

The harder question is not whether to go back. It is what to do with the time. And this is where most FIRE literature fails you completely, because it treats "what to do with your time" as self-evident. It is not self-evident. It is, in fact, the entire question. Money solves the financial problem. It does not solve the meaning problem. Those are two different problems and you need two different solutions.

錢解決了財務問題。
錢解決不了意義的問題。
這是兩個不同的問題，需要兩個不同的答案。

On Embracing Freedom Without Apologising For It

The freedom is real. This is worth stating plainly, because the guilt can make you forget it. You made deliberate choices over many years. You delayed gratification. You built something. The freedom you now have is not luck — it is the compound interest of thousands of small decisions made correctly over a long time. You are allowed to enjoy it. You are, in fact, obligated to enjoy it. Wasting freedom is its own kind of waste, and arguably a worse one than wasting money.

The guilt is not evidence that you've done something wrong. It is evidence that you were conditioned, for decades, to measure your worth in output. That conditioning does not disappear the day you stop producing. It fades. Slowly. Give it time. Go for the Tuesday walk. You have earned the Tuesday walk.

罪惡感不是你做錯了的證明。
它只是幾十年的制約留下的痕跡。
去散步吧。週二早上的散步，你值得。

You are not too young to have stopped. You are exactly the right age — the age at which you had the resources, the clarity, and the courage to make the choice. Most people never get there. Not because they couldn't, but because they never decided to. You decided. That decision deserves respect, including from yourself. Especially from yourself.

The question is not whether to go back to a corporate job. The question is what kind of person you want to be in this new chapter — and what that person spends their Tuesday mornings doing. That is a much more interesting question. It turns out it is also a much harder one. Welcome to the real work.

FIRE is not retirement in the traditional sense. It is not a hammock and a piña colada and the end of all productive thought. It is, if done correctly, the beginning of the first chapter you actually chose to write. The previous chapters were good. They funded this one. But this one is yours — unscheduled, unoptimised, and entirely up to you.

The guilt will pass. The freedom will remain. Learn to tell the difference between the two.

罪惡感會過去的。
自由會留下來。
學著分清楚這兩件事。
然後，好好過你的週二早上。
