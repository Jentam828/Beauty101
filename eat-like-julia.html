Eat Like Julia

像茱莉亞一樣吃

A dinner guide for your out-of-town guest visiting San Francisco — ordered, explained, and fully endorsed by a 6'2" former spy who learned to cook in Paris and never looked back.

Food· 10 min read · 十分鐘· Entry No. 002

✦ ✦ ✦

Your friend is visiting from out of town. They want a proper French dinner in San Francisco. You want to impress them without pretending you know more than you do. Good news: Julia Child has already done the homework. All you have to do is follow her lead. And maybe not knock anything off the table.

朋友從外地來，想吃一頓正式的法式晚餐。
你想讓他們印象深刻，但又不想裝作自己是美食專家。
放心。茱莉亞·柴爾德已經幫你做好功課了。

Before she was America's most beloved cooking teacher, Julia Child was a 6'2" California girl from Pasadena who couldn't boil an egg. She joined the OSS — the precursor to the CIA — during World War II after being rejected from the military for being too tall. Her first assignment involved developing shark repellent. Yes, really. She was posted to Ceylon and then Kunming, China, where she handled top-secret intelligence and, somewhere along the way, met her husband Paul.

When Paul was assigned to Paris in 1948, Julia ate her first proper French meal — sole meunière at a restaurant in Rouen — and described it as an opening up of the soul. She enrolled at Le Cordon Bleu, became a serious student of French cuisine, and spent the next decade writing Mastering the Art of French Cooking, which changed how Americans cooked. She passed away in 2004, two days before her 92nd birthday, with a bowl of French onion soup on her bedside table. Of course.

茱莉亞·柴爾德出生於加州帕薩迪納，身高六呎二。
她不是廚師出身，而是前情報官員——真正的那種。
她在中國雲南昆明處理過最高機密文件。
後來嫁給了在巴黎吃遍美食的丈夫，這才開始學做飯。
有時候，人生的轉彎來得很晚，但來得剛剛好。

First, Where Are You Going?

San Francisco has no shortage of French restaurants. But tonight, we're going to Frances on 17th Street in the Castro — a Michelin-starred neighbourhood restaurant that doesn't feel like it's trying to impress you, which is exactly why it does. Less white tablecloth, more good food. Julia would have liked it immediately.

Frances

3870 17th St, San Francisco · The Castro · Michelin Starred · $$ · Tue–Sat from 5:15pm

Named after chef-owner Melissa Perello's grandmother — which is already a good sign — Frances opened in 2009 and earned its Michelin star without any fuss. The menu changes seasonally, sometimes weekly, built around whatever the Northern California farmers markets are doing that day. Californian produce with a Mediterranean bent, rustic but precise — no trendy powders or foams in sight. Book ahead. Reservations are difficult to score and walk-ins are a gamble you probably don't want to take with an out-of-town guest.

You're seated. The bread has arrived. Your guest is looking at the menu with that particular expression people get when everything sounds good and nothing makes sense. This is where Julia steps in.

"The secret of a happy marriage is finding the right person.
You know they're right if you love to be with them all the time —
and you love to be with them especially when you're eating."
— Julia Child，她說的是婚姻，但也可能是說法國菜。
一
Appetizer · 前菜
Seared Foie Gras
煎鵝肝

Start here. Foie gras is the kind of dish people either love immediately or spend ten minutes explaining why they don't eat it. Either way, it makes for good dinner conversation. Seared gives you a caramelised crust with a silky centre — the contrast is the whole point. Do not skip the brioche it comes with. That is not a suggestion.

Julia learned her foie gras in Paris, where it was treated not as indulgence but as craft. In her years at Le Cordon Bleu, she understood that French cooking was never about excess — it was about respect for the ingredient. "Find something you're passionate about and keep tremendously interested in it," she once said. For foie gras, she clearly was.
🍷
Wine Pairing · 酒款搭配
Sauternes — Château d'Yquem
Sweet, golden, complex. The classic pairing. Foie gras and Sauternes is not a suggestion — it's a law Julia would have happily enforced.
二
Salad · 沙拉
Roasted Beet Salad with Chèvre
烤甜菜沙拉佐山羊起司

Between the richness of foie gras and whatever is coming next, you need something that reminds your palate that vegetables exist. Roasted beets with goat cheese do this gracefully. The earthiness of the beet, the tang of the chèvre, a handful of walnuts for texture. Simple. Effective. Nobody ever regrets the beet salad.

Julia spent time in Germany when Paul was stationed there in the early 1950s. Root vegetables — beets, turnips, parsnips — were staples she came to respect. She brought that appreciation back to her French cooking, understanding that peasant ingredients, treated properly, can outperform fancy ones. The beet is living proof.
🥂
Wine Pairing · 酒款搭配
Sancerre — Henri Bourgeois
Crisp Loire Valley Sauvignon Blanc. Cuts through the goat cheese beautifully. Refreshes without competing.
三
Main Course · 主菜
Sole Meunière
檸檬奶油比目魚

This is the one. If you order nothing else on this list, order this. Sole meunière is lightly floured fish, pan-fried in butter, finished with lemon and parsley. It sounds almost insultingly simple. It is not. Done correctly, it is one of the finest things you can eat. Done incorrectly, it tastes like a gym cafeteria. Frances does not make gym cafeteria food.

This was Julia's first real French meal. In 1948, newly arrived in France, she sat down at La Couronne in Rouen and ordered sole meunière. She described the experience to the New York Times as an opening up of the soul and spirit. A 6'2" former intelligence officer from Pasadena, reduced to a profound moment of clarity over a piece of fish in butter. That is the power of French cooking. And also of good butter.
🍾
Wine Pairing · 酒款搭配
Burgundy White — Meursault Premier Cru
Rich, nutty Chardonnay from Burgundy. Matches the butter in the dish without overwhelming the delicate fish. Julia's choice. Obviously.
四
Cheese · 起司
The Cheese Cart
起司車

In France, cheese comes before dessert. Not after. This is not negotiable and also the correct way. Frances's cheese selection rotates with the season — ask your server what's on. As a general rule: one soft, one semi-firm, one aged. Do not take more than three. You are not at a buffet.

Julia embraced the French cheese course completely and without apology. During her Paris years, she educated herself on every French region's contribution — Normandy's Camembert, Burgundy's Époisses, the Pyrenees' Ossau-Iraty. She understood that cheese was not an afterthought. It was a bridge between the savoury and the sweet. The French figured this out centuries ago. We're still catching up.
🍷
Wine Pairing · 酒款搭配
Côtes du Rhône Rouge — Château Rayas
A medium-bodied red with enough structure for aged cheese, enough elegance not to bully the soft ones. Works with everything on the cart.
五
Dessert · 甜點
Crème Brûlée
法式焦糖燉蛋

End here. Always end with crème brûlée at a French restaurant, for the same reason you always end a conversation with a good joke — it's the last thing people remember. Crack the top. Listen for the sound. If it doesn't make a sound, send it back. You are allowed to do this. Julia would have.

In her later years, Julia simplified her cooking considerably. The elaborate multi-step recipes of Mastering the Art of French Cooking gave way to a more relaxed approach — good ingredients, proper technique, don't fuss. Crème brûlée embodies this perfectly. Cream, eggs, sugar, vanilla, heat. Nothing hidden. Everything matters. Like most things worth doing.
🥂
Wine Pairing · 酒款搭配
Champagne — Billecart-Salmon Blanc de Blancs
Crisp, bright, celebratory. End the meal the way Julia would have ended any good evening — with Champagne and no apologies.

One Last Thing

Julia Child spent her life teaching Americans that French food was not intimidating, not pretentious, and not beyond reach. She did this from a kitchen in Cambridge, Massachusetts, on public television, with a budget that would make modern food media weep. She was also, somewhere in her past, a woman who flew over the Himalayas on a C-46 and handled top-secret documents in Kunming, China. She called the war the thing that made her. French cooking was what she did with that making.

Order the sole meunière. Drink the Meursault. Crack the crème brûlée. Your out-of-town guest will think you know exactly what you're doing. You don't have to tell them you had help.

點比目魚。喝白酒。敲碎焦糖布蕾的頂部，聽那個聲音。
你的朋友會以為你是美食專家。
你不需要告訴他們，你只是跟著茱莉亞走。
