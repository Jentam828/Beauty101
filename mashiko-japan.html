Mashiko, Japan

日本 · 益子

A town built around clay, fire, and the patience to turn both into something beautiful. For someone who just started making ceramics, this was not optional.

🏺 Japan Trip · Stop Two · Oct 20–22, 2025

Travels· 7 min read · 七分鐘· Entry No. 002

🏺 🍂 🌾

I started taking ceramics lessons a few months ago. I am not good at it yet. The clay has its own opinions and they frequently differ from mine. But somewhere between the centering and the pulling and the quiet concentration that throwing on a wheel demands, I understood why people spend their lives doing this. So when the Japan itinerary was taking shape, Mashiko was not a question. It was the answer.

我最近開始學陶藝。我還不太擅長。
泥土有自己的想法，常常和我的不一樣。
但就在拉坯的過程中，我明白了為什麼有人願意一生做這件事。
所以益子，不是選項——是答案。

Mashiko is a small town in Tochigi Prefecture, about 80 kilometres north of Tokyo. Pottery has been made here since the Jōmon period — roughly 10,000 years ago — but Mashiko's modern identity as a ceramics town dates to the mid-19th century, when a local potter named Keizaburo Otsuka established a kiln here in 1853, drawn by the quality of the local clay.

The town's international reputation came largely through one man: Shoji Hamada, who settled in Mashiko in 1924 and spent the rest of his life there. Hamada was a central figure in the mingei — folk craft — movement, a philosophy that championed the beauty of everyday, handmade objects over the precious and the ornate. He became a Living National Treasure of Japan in 1955. His workshop and home are now part of the Mashiko Museum of Ceramic Art, and Mashiko's reputation as one of Japan's most important pottery towns is built substantially on his legacy.

益子是栃木縣的小鎮，距東京約80公里。
縄文時代起便有陶藝歷史，現代窯業從1853年興起。
陶藝家濱田庄司於1924年定居益子，推廣民藝運動，1955年獲選「人間國寶」。
他的故居與工坊現為益子陶藝美術館的一部分。

The Walk In

I walked from the town centre to the Mashikokan Satoyama Resort Hotel. It is a longer walk than the map suggests, which is fine because the map does not account for stopping. And you will stop. The road out of town is lined with persimmon trees heavy with fruit — deep orange against grey October sky — and farmland running alongside, vegetables and late flowers still holding on. It is the kind of walk that makes you wonder why you don't walk more. Then you remember you live somewhere with hills and no persimmon trees, and the feeling passes.

從市中心走到旅館，比地圖上看起來要遠。
但沒關係，因為路上會一直停下來。
柿子樹結滿了果實，深橘色掛在十月的灰色天空下。
農田延伸在路旁，蔬菜和晚開的花還在撐著。

Mashiko Museum of Ceramic Art

The Mashiko Museum of Ceramic Art — Mashiko Sankokan — was established in 1992 and sits on grounds that include Shoji Hamada's original studio, kiln, and residence, relocated and preserved here. The museum's permanent collection covers Hamada's own work as well as pieces by Bernard Leach, the British potter who was Hamada's lifelong collaborator and friend, and rotating exhibitions of contemporary ceramics.

The buildings themselves are worth the visit — traditional Japanese structures set among trees, the kind of place that moves slowly and rewards patience. The actual kilns are visible. The workshop where Hamada made pots that ended up in museums around the world is still there, unchanged. Standing in it feels like standing in working history rather than preserved history, which is a meaningful distinction.

益子陶藝美術館成立於1992年，保留了濱田庄司的原始工坊、窯與故居。
館藏包括濱田與英國陶藝家伯納德·利奇的作品，以及當代陶藝展覽。
站在那個工坊裡，感覺不像在參觀歷史，而像是歷史還在運作中。
If you've just started making pottery, standing in Hamada's studio is either deeply inspiring or mildly humbling, depending on your disposition. I was both. His pots are simple in the way that things made with total mastery appear simple. Nothing wasted. Nothing showing off. I went back twice.
The main street is everything you want it to be — ceramic shops, antique shops, cafés, and the quiet understanding that everyone here has come for the same reason and nobody needs to explain themselves.
主街道正是你所期待的樣子。沒有人需要解釋自己為什麼來。

Mashikokan Satoyama Resort Hotel

The hotel sits outside of town in its own green silence. The room is spacious — genuinely spacious, not hotel-brochure spacious — and the grounds have the unhurried feeling of somewhere that has been here long enough to stop trying. The hot spring baths, both indoor and open-air, are exactly what two days of walking and ceramics browsing require. The open-air bath in the evening, surrounded by trees going dark, is one of those experiences you don't fully appreciate until you are back home in your regular shower wondering what happened.

Dinner is on the expensive side. It is also very good. These two facts coexist without contradiction and you should budget accordingly rather than being surprised at the end.

The in-room massage sounds appealing after a long day. Skip it. Book the spa room instead — the proper treatment room with a real table and the full setup. The difference is significant enough that I'm mentioning it here rather than keeping it to myself.

客房內的按摩聽起來很吸引人。跳過它。
請預訂正式的Spa房——差別很大，值得特別提一下。

A Day in Mashiko — If You Only Have One

9:00 AM
Mashiko Museum of Ceramic Art
Start here before the tour groups arrive. Allow 90 minutes minimum — more if you find yourself lingering in Hamada's studio, which you will. 趁人潮到來前先參觀。至少留90分鐘，很可能更久。
Entry fee applies. Check current hours before you go.
11:00 AM
Ceramic Shops on the Main Street
The main street (Jōnachi) is lined with galleries and shops selling work by local potters. Budget more time than you think — and more luggage space than you packed. Things will follow you home. 主街道上滿是在地陶藝家的作品。準備比你預期更多的時間——和行李空間。
Most shops are happy for you to browse slowly. Nobody rushes you.
1:00 PM
Lunch at Café Novel
About a 15-minute walk from the main street — worth every step. The kind of place that makes lunch feel like the main event rather than the break between shopping. Simple, well-made food, good coffee, and the particular charm of a café that has no interest in being anything other than what it is. 距主街道步行約15分鐘——每一步都值得。食物用心，咖啡好喝，氣氛剛剛好。強烈推薦。
No reservations taken — arrive early or be prepared to wait a little.
2:30 PM
Antique Shops
Mashiko has an improbable number of antique shops tucked between and behind the ceramic galleries. Ceramics, lacquerware, old textiles, things you cannot name but cannot leave without. Allow the afternoon. 益子的古董店多得令人意外，藏在陶藝店之間。下午就留給這些吧。
4:30 PM
Cake & Coffee — Any of the Local Cafés
Sit down. You've been on your feet all day. The cafés along the main street are uniformly charming and the cake is consistently better than it needs to be. 坐下來。走了一整天了。主街道上的咖啡廳都很迷人，蛋糕都比預期好吃。
6:30 PM
Dinner at Mashikokan Satoyama Resort Hotel
Reserve when you book the room. The dinner menu is expensive and very good. The hot spring bath before dinner, or after, is the correct ending to a day like this. 訂房時同步預訂晚餐。價格偏高，但品質對得起。飯前或飯後泡溫泉，都是完美的結尾。
And book the spa room, not the in-room massage. You'll thank yourself later.

Mashiko Pottery Festival

益子陶器市集
📅 Late April & Early November · 每年四月下旬與十一月上旬

I missed it. My trip was October 20–22 and the autumn festival runs in early November — close enough to be annoying, far enough to be a legitimate reason to come back. The Mashiko Pottery Festival is held twice a year, in late April (Golden Week) and early November, and draws over 500 potters from across Japan selling directly from temporary stalls set up around the town.

If you are at all interested in ceramics — or in the particular pleasure of buying things directly from the person who made them — plan your trip around the festival dates. The town is busier but the payoff is significant. This is on my list for the next trip. The clay and I still have things to settle.

我錯過了。十一月上旬的陶器市集就在我離開後不久舉辦。
每年四月和十一月各一次，超過500位陶藝家在此擺攤。
如果你對陶藝有任何興趣，請配合市集日期安排行程。
我已經把下次的行程排好了。我和泥土之間，還有些事情沒解決。
