One Fine Day in SF

舊金山美好的一天

Early spring. Best friend in town. High of 70°F, no fog, comfortable breeze. San Francisco performing at its absolute best — and a day designed to match it.

🌤 Early Spring  ·  High 70°F  ·  No Fog  ·  Light Breeze

Travels· 8 min read · 八分鐘· Entry No. 003

🌉 🌿 🥂

There are two San Franciscos. The one tourists see — the bridges, the cable cars, the sourdough bowl at Fisherman's Wharf — and the one you fall in love with when someone who actually lives there takes you around. This was a day of the second kind. My best friend was in from out of town. The weather had decided to cooperate for once. And we had no plan beyond a loose sequence of places we wanted to be, people we wanted to see, and food we intended to eat. Which is exactly the right amount of plan for a city like this.

舊金山有兩種。遊客看到的那種，和真正住在這裡的人帶你去的那種。
這一天屬於第二種。
最好的朋友來訪。天氣難得配合。計劃鬆散得恰到好處。

San Francisco is one of the few American cities
where not having a car is not a compromise.
It is a lifestyle choice that rewards you constantly.
舊金山是少數幾個沒有車不是妥協，而是一種選擇的美國城市。

First Stop: Frugality with Good Taste

🪑
Morning · Thrifting
GoodWill
1215 California St, Burlingame, CA

There is a particular satisfaction that comes from finding something genuinely good for almost nothing. Two chairs. Under two dollars each. The kind of find that makes you feel simultaneously lucky and slightly superior — not in a mean way, just in the quiet way of someone who knows something others walked past. It set the tone for the whole day: paying attention, moving slowly, keeping your eyes open.

Brunch and a Neighbourhood Walk

🌮
Brunch · Mexican · 4.7★
el Mil Amores
2780 21st St, San Francisco · Open daily 8am–3pm

The name means "a thousand loves" and the café de olla alone is worth at least a hundred of them — sweet, spiced, and the kind of warm that a 70-degree SF morning still calls for. El Mil Amores is the kind of neighbourhood spot that is exactly what you want brunch to be: unhurried, genuinely good, unpretentious, and with a menu where everything sounds right. The CDMX plate — chilaquiles, eggs, potatoes, and concha French toast — is the kind of breakfast that makes the rest of the day feel like a gift.

There is usually a wait. It is worth it. Bring patience or arrive early. They do not take reservations and they do not need to.

After brunch, we walked. This is the correct thing to do in Potrero Hill — a neighbourhood that rewards the pedestrian with good views, quiet streets, and the mild smugness of being somewhere that hasn't been fully discovered yet. SF is a walkable city in the truest sense: not because everything is flat (it is famously not), but because every neighbourhood has its own logic, its own pace, and something worth slowing down for.

🐾
Drop-In · Senior Dog Rescue · 4.8★
Muttville Senior Dog Rescue
750 Florida St, San Francisco · Tue–Sun 11am–4pm

Muttville rescues senior dogs — the ones most shelters overlook in favour of puppies — and finds them homes for whatever time they have left. It is one of the more quietly moving organisations in the city. You go in to look at cute dogs. You come out thinking about what it means to give something a good ending. We did not adopt anyone, which required more restraint than I care to admit.

🏺
Window Shopping · Ceramics · 4.7★
Heath Ceramics
2900 18th St, San Francisco · Mon–Fri 10am–6pm · Sat–Sun 10am–5pm

Every piece is beautiful. This is not an exaggeration — it is a design philosophy that Heath has maintained since Edith Heath founded the company in Sausalito in 1948. Muted glazes, clean lines, the kind of ceramics that make whatever food you put in them look considered. We admired everything. We bought nothing. Our wallets made the final call, which they frequently do at Heath. This is not a complaint. Window shopping at Heath is its own pleasure — it costs nothing and you leave with better taste than when you walked in.

🧀
Grocery · Worker-Owned Co-op · 4.6★
Rainbow Grocery Cooperative
1745 Folsom St, San Francisco · Daily 9am–9pm

Rainbow is worker-owned, vegetarian, and one of the best grocery stores in the city by a comfortable margin. The bulk section alone — herbs, grains, teas, nuts, chocolate — could occupy a serious person for the better part of an hour. We came for cheese, chocolate, and kitchen towels. We left with cheese, chocolate, kitchen towels, and several things we had not planned on but could not justify leaving behind. This is always how Rainbow goes. Budget accordingly.

Into Nature — The SF Version

One of the things people outside of SF don't always appreciate is how much green is folded into the city. Not just Golden Gate Park — though that is extraordinary — but neighbourhood gardens, urban farms, community plots, and the general insistence that plants belong in cities as much as people do. The afternoon was built around this idea.

🌱
Urban Farm · Community Garden · 4.9★
Garden for the Environment
1590 7th Ave, Sunset District · Daily 7am–6:30pm · Free

Hidden in the Sunset on 7th Avenue, Garden for the Environment is a working urban demonstration garden — a teaching space for sustainable gardening, composting, and food growing in a city context. Beehives, vegetable beds, native plants, outdoor classrooms, and an atmosphere of quiet productivity that feels completely removed from the city surrounding it. Entry is free. It is genuinely one of SF's underappreciated gems.

If you have any interest in urban farming, edible plants, or simply the idea that a city can grow its own food, this is where that interest gets concrete. The volunteers who tend it clearly love every square foot. That care is visible in every corner.

🌳
Park · Botanical Garden · 4.8★
Golden Gate Park & SF Botanical Garden
1199 9th Ave, San Francisco · Daily 7:30am–5pm · Free for SF residents

Golden Gate Park is 1,017 acres, which is larger than Central Park, a fact New Yorkers find inconvenient. A relaxed stroll through it on a 70-degree spring afternoon, with no agenda beyond moving through trees and air, is one of the better uses of an afternoon in this city. The SF Botanical Garden sits within the park and contains over 9,000 plant species from around the world — arranged by region, labelled clearly, and maintained with evident care.

In early spring, the magnolias are the reason to go. They are magnificent in the literal sense — large, architectural, and in full bloom before most other trees have decided to wake up.

Magnolia flowers are edible and have been used in tea for centuries — particularly in traditional Chinese and Japanese medicine. The petals have a mild, slightly floral and gingery flavour. Next time you walk past one in bloom, you're looking at a cup of tea. A very beautiful, extremely temporary cup of tea.

木蘭花可以入茶——在中日傳統醫學中使用已有幾個世紀。
花瓣帶有淡淡的花香和薑的味道。下次看到盛開的木蘭，你看到的是一杯茶。

The Right Ending

🍝
Dinner · Sardinian · 4.6★
La Ciccia
291 30th St, San Francisco · Tue–Sat 5:30pm–9:45pm · Closed Sun & Mon

La Ciccia is a small, family-run Sardinian restaurant on 30th Street in Noe Valley — one of those neighbourhood restaurants that has quietly become essential without making a fuss about it. The octopus stew, the gnocchi, the pasta: all of it tastes like someone's grandmother made it, if your grandmother happened to be from Sardinia and have exceptional technique. The room is cozy. The service is warm. The prices are honest for what you get.

We ended the day here. After the thrifting and the brunch and the dogs and the ceramics and the urban farm and the magnolias, we sat down and ate well and talked the way you only talk with someone you've known long enough that no topic requires an introduction. That is the best thing a city can do — give you the right container for a day worth having. SF, on this particular day, did that completely.

San Francisco gets a lot wrong. Housing. Cost of living. The fog that arrives in June and forgets to leave until September. But on an early spring day with a best friend and no particular agenda, it does everything right. The neighbourhoods connect on foot. The food is genuinely good across every price point. The parks are serious parks. And the people who love this city love it in a way that is infectious and specific and not quite reproducible anywhere else.

Two chairs under four dollars. Chilaquiles. Senior dogs. Magnolia tea. La Ciccia at dusk. One fine day.

舊金山有很多做得不好的地方。房價。生活成本。六月來了不走的霧。
但在一個早春的日子裡，帶著最好的朋友，沒有特別的計劃——
它把一切都做對了。
兩把椅子不到四美元。木蘭花。老狗。La Ciccia 的章魚燉。
美好的一天。
