Istanbul, Turkey

土耳其 · 伊斯坦堡

Seven days between two continents, in the care of generous friends, eating and wandering and occasionally walking into places we had absolutely no business being in.

Travels· 10 min read · 十分鐘· April 16–22, 2026

📅 Published · April 25, 2026
🕌 🌊 🫖

Let me tell you something about Istanbul. It does not ease you in gently. From the moment you arrive, the city is simply happening — all of it, all at once, the minarets and the ferries and the smell of simit on the morning air and the particular light off the Bosphorus that makes everything look like it has been painted by someone who understood exactly what they were doing. You either surrender to it or you don't. We surrendered immediately, and it was the best decision we made.

讓我告訴你關於伊斯坦堡的事。它不會輕輕地把你帶入。
一到那裡，城市就是在發生——全部，同時，
清真寺的尖塔，輪渡，清晨空氣中的芝麻圈香氣，
還有博斯普魯斯海峽上那種光，讓一切都像是被懂得光線的人畫出來的。
你要麼臣服，要麼不。我們立刻臣服了。

We were lucky — and I do not use that word lightly — to have David, who lives in Istanbul, as our guide and our host. He and Alberto opened their apartment to Paul and me for the week, and what an apartment it was: a front-row seat overlooking the Istanbul Modern museum and the water, in a neighbourhood that hums with the particular energy of somewhere that knows it is having a moment and is entirely comfortable with that. Trendy cafés, Turkish coffee served in small glasses, tea at every turn, simit vendors on the corner. We unpacked, looked out the window at the Bosphorus, and agreed that this was going to be a very good week.

餐桌與街道

The Table & The Streets

— 🍽 —

Food in Istanbul is not just sustenance. It is the argument the city makes for itself — the mezes arriving in waves, the bread warm from the oven, the raki poured generously, the view through the window always somehow better than expected. We ate very well. We ate together. We ate in the way that travel is supposed to make you eat — slowly, gratefully, with nowhere else to be.

🍷
Meyhane · Traditional Tavern · Beyoğlu
Demeti Meyhanesi
傳統土耳其小館 · 貝伊奧盧區
Şimşirci Sok. No:6, Cihangir-Beyoğlu, Istanbul

A meyhane is not simply a restaurant. It is a state of mind — an old Turkish tavern tradition where the evening unfolds slowly over shared plates of meze, a bottle of raki, and conversation that has nowhere particular to go. Demeti is the first meyhane in the Cihangir district, and it wears its history without fuss. The cozy, homey atmosphere, with cabinets filled with antiques and celebrity portraits rubbing shoulders with family photos on the walls, opens onto a small balcony with a view of the Bosphorus that stops you mid-sentence.

We chose our mezes from the counter — pickled tomatoes, pomegranate salad, börek filled with fish and eggplant and cheese, shrimp casserole — and ate the way the place asks you to eat: without hurry, with appreciation, watching the boats move across the water through the window. It felt, entirely, like someone's family dining room. In the best possible way.

One of the finest meals of the trip. The food is delicious, the service warm, and the view through those windows is the kind that makes you put your fork down and just look for a moment. Reserve ahead. This place deserves to be found, and other people have found it.
☕
Café · Cihangir Neighbourhood
Susam Cafe & Kahve 6
蘇薩姆咖啡 & 卡赫維6號 · 吉漢吉爾區
Cihangir, Beyoğlu, Istanbul

Cihangir is the neighbourhood where Istanbul's artists, writers and expats have quietly congregated for decades — the kind of area where every café has a personality, the streets are steep and handsome, and you can spend a very pleasant morning doing nothing more ambitious than drinking coffee and watching the neighbourhood go about its business. Susam Cafe and Kahve 6 both deliver exactly what you want from a morning in Cihangir: good coffee, good light, good company. Shared meals, unhurried conversations, the city below.

渡輪 · 亞洲那側

Across the Water — The Asian Side

— ⛴ —
⛴️
Ferry Crossing · Bosphorus
Ferry to the Asian Side
渡輪 · 博斯普魯斯海峽 · 亞洲
Eminönü to Kadıköy or Üsküdar · approx. 20 min

There is something almost philosophical about the Istanbul ferry crossing — the act of sitting on a boat in a glass of tea, watching one continent recede as another approaches. The Asian side receives you differently: quieter in feeling, the shops more local, the restaurants full of residents rather than tourists, the back alleys worth every wrong turn. We wandered for hours. We ate. We looked in shop windows. We walked through streets that had no intention of impressing us and were therefore, entirely, impressive.

Istanbul is the only city in the world that sits on two continents.
Once you have crossed between them on a ferry with a glass of tea in your hand,
every other city feels like it is missing something.
伊斯坦堡是世界上唯一橫跨兩個大陸的城市。
一旦你手持茶杯乘渡輪往返兩岸，其他城市都會讓你覺得缺少了什麼。
🚪
The Best Accident of the Trip · 最美麗的意外
The Lady with the Tea — Bi Nevi Deli, Beşiktaş
那位端茶出來的女士 · 貝希克塔什區
Etiler, Dilhayat Sk. No:10/1, 34337 Beşiktaş, Istanbul

I need to tell you about the hill. After the ferry, Istanbul's traffic being what Istanbul's traffic is, we decided to walk to Bi Nevi Deli — a journey that involved approximately fifteen minutes of climbing a gradient that a city with less confidence would describe as a cliff. We arrived at the top, thirsty and slightly winded, and looked up to find a beautiful space with outdoor seating overlooking the town. Exactly what we needed. We walked in.

A smiling woman appeared, showed us to the outdoor seats, and brought tea, water, and cookies. We sat. We drank. We had a second round of tea. We were restored. When we finally moved to pay the bill, we discovered, gently and with some embarrassment, that there was no bill. There was no café. What we had walked into — helped ourselves to the outdoor seating of, accepted the hospitality of — was, in fact, an office. Possibly a private residence. We had, quite thoroughly, intruded on someone's home.

The woman, for her part, seemed entirely unbothered. She had seen two tired strangers arrive at her door — David, Paul, and me — and had simply done what the best version of hospitality asks: offered what she had, asked nothing in return. We left more grateful than words can comfortably hold. That, I think, is Turkey in a single moment.

We did eventually make it to Bi Nevi Deli. But I will remember the accidental stop far longer than the destination. Some of the best moments in travel are the ones you could never have planned — the woman at the top of the hill, the tea, the cookies, the complete absence of any transaction. Pure, unreasonable kindness. Istanbul gave us that, and I will not forget it.
土耳其浴 · 療癒

Sunday Morning — The Hamam

— 🛁 —
💧
Turkish Bath · 16th Century · Tophane
Kılıç Ali Paşa Hamamı
克勒奇·阿里帕夏浴場 · 托法內區
Kemankeş Mah. Hamam Sok. No:1, Karaköy, Beyoğlu, Istanbul

The Kılıç Ali Paşa Hamamı was built by the great Ottoman architect Mimar Sinan between 1578 and 1580, commissioned by Kılıç Ali Pasha — hailed as the "conqueror of the seas" — to serve the mariners of Tophane, Istanbul's seafront district. The man himself was born Giovanni Dionigi Galeni, a Christian boy from Calabria, Italy, who was captured by Ottoman forces, converted to Islam, rose to become Grand Admiral of the Ottoman fleet, and crossed paths with Miguel de Cervantes in Algiers — who later paid tribute to him in Don Quixote. You do not simply come here to wash. You come here to sit inside several centuries of history and let the steam do its work.

The hamam reopened in 2012 after seven years of meticulous restoration, receiving a Europa Nostra award for outstanding achievement in heritage preservation. Under the great marble dome, in the warm silence of the caldarium, with the light falling through the star-shaped apertures above, a deep tissue massage delivers the particular kind of surrender that only a place like this can produce. Heavenly is not too strong a word.

Reserve well in advance — this is not a secret. But nothing about the experience feels touristic once you are inside. The marble, the steam, the dome, the attendants who clearly understand what they are doing — all of it adds up to something that sits outside the normal categories of spa and sightseeing. It is its own thing entirely. Do not skip it.
歷史遺蹟

The Monuments — Humbled & Grateful

— 🕌 —
🕌
UNESCO World Heritage · Sultanahmet
The Blue Mosque & Hagia Sophia Grand Mosque
藍色清真寺 & 聖索菲亞大清真寺 · 蘇丹艾哈邁德區
Sultanahmet, Fatih, Istanbul

There are places that photographs simply cannot prepare you for — not because the photographs are bad, but because the photographs can only capture what something looks like, and these places are about what they feel like. The Blue Mosque, with its six minarets and its cascade of domes, is one. The Hagia Sophia, which has been a basilica, a mosque, a museum, and a mosque again across nearly fifteen centuries, is another. You stand inside them and feel, without quite being able to articulate why, both very small and very lucky to be there.

We were not religious pilgrims, but the reverence required of visitors here is not merely procedural — it is a genuine response to what these buildings carry. Centuries of prayer, of history, of human ambition directed toward something larger than itself. We walked the grounds slowly. We looked up often. We were humbled.

Seven days is not enough for Istanbul. It never could be. But with the right people — and we had the right people — seven days can give you enough to understand why this city gets under your skin and stays there. The food, the history, the water, the light, the woman at the top of the hill with her tea and her inexhaustible generosity — all of it.

David and Alberto: thank you. For opening your home, for showing us your Istanbul rather than the tourist version of it, for the meals and the walks and the ferry and the laughter. Paul and I came as guests and left as people who feel they have been given something they cannot fully repay. That is the best kind of travel, and it only happens because of the best kind of friends.

七天對伊斯坦堡來說不夠。永遠不夠。
但有對的人陪伴——我們有——七天已足夠讓你明白這座城市為何讓人難以忘懷。
食物、歷史、海水、光線，還有那位山頂上捧茶而來的女士。
David 和 Alberto：謝謝你們。
為我們開門，帶我們看屬於你們的伊斯坦堡，而不是觀光版本的。
Paul 和我以客人的身份來，以受到無可回報饋贈的人的身份離開。
這是最好的旅行，只因為有最好的朋友。
