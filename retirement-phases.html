The Three Phases of Early Retirement

提早退休的三個階段

A map for the road ahead. Not a guarantee — maps never are — but a way of thinking about time, money, and what you want to do with both before you no longer can.

Personal Therapy· 9 min read · 九分鐘· Entry No. 003

🟢 🔵 🟣

Nobody talks about retirement the way it actually unfolds. The financial planning industry hands you a number — save this much, withdraw this percentage, you'll be fine — and treats the next thirty years as a single, undifferentiated block of time. But retirement isn't one thing. It's three very different things, happening sequentially, each with its own energy, its own demands, and its own price tag. Getting the map wrong doesn't just cost you money. It costs you time. And time, unlike money, cannot be earned back.

沒有人用它真實的樣子談退休。
理財顧問給你一個數字，然後把接下來三十年當成一整塊來規劃。
但退休不是一件事。它是三件截然不同的事，按順序發生，
各有各的精力、各有各的需求、各有各的價格。

The framework is simple. Financial planners have known it for years, though they don't always lead with it. Your retirement happens in three phases — Go-Go, Slow-Go, and No-Go — and understanding the shape of each one changes how you think about both your money and your days. Especially if you've retired early. Especially if you live somewhere expensive. Especially if you're honest with yourself about what the numbers actually mean.

"The goal of investing isn't to maximize returns.
It's to maximize the odds of not running out of money
before you run out of time."
— 投資的目標不是最大化報酬。而是最大化在時間耗盡之前，錢不會先耗盡的機率。
🏃 Phase
One
Ages 55–69 · 第一階段
The Go-Go Years
55 to 69 · 15 years · 十五年

This is the phase most people picture when they imagine retirement — and they are right to. Your body still does what you ask it to. Your curiosity is intact. You have the energy to go places, try things, and come back wanting more. The Go-Go years are the ones you spend, in the best sense of the word.

The mistake people make here is under-spending, out of anxiety about the years ahead. But there is a real cost to saving your best years for later — later has a way of arriving with different capabilities than you planned for. The Go-Go years are when international travel is still genuinely comfortable, when learning something new still feels like growth rather than effort, when a part-time job is a choice rather than a necessity.

Activities · 活動
  • At least 2 international trips per year — this is the window 每年至少兩次國際旅行——這是最佳時機
  • Road trips through California and beyond — flexible, spontaneous 加州及周邊公路旅行——靈活、自在
  • Volunteering — giving time when time is abundant 志工服務——時間充裕時，給予時間
  • Classes for self-improvement — language, ceramics, cooking, whatever calls 自我提升課程——語言、陶藝、烹飪，任何吸引你的
  • Part-time work on your own terms — for stimulation, not survival 兼職工作，按自己的條件——為了刺激感，不是為了生存
Money · 財務
Annual Budget · Bay Area
Housing, travel (2 international + several road trips), classes, dining, healthcare, daily life in one of the most expensive regions in the US. This is not extravagant — it is realistic for the Bay Area.
$100K per year × 15 years = $1,500,000
🚶 Phase
Two
Ages 70–79 · 第二階段
The Slow-Go Years
70 to 79 · 10 years · 十年

The pace changes. Not dramatically — this isn't decline, it's recalibration. You still want to be out in the world, still want to learn, still want to grow things and be with people. But the appetite for jet lag decreases. The appeal of a long haul flight to somewhere unfamiliar diminishes relative to the appeal of somewhere known, comfortable, and close.

The Slow-Go years are underrated. There is a particular quality to life at 70 that the Go-Go years don't have — a deeper comfort with yourself, a less anxious relationship with time, a genuine pleasure in smaller pleasures. Growing herbs on a balcony. A book club that meets every month with the same people. One carefully chosen trip instead of two rushed ones.

Activities · 活動
  • One international trip per year — chosen with more care, enjoyed more fully 每年一次國際旅行——更謹慎挑選，更充分享受
  • Local road trips — the familiar places, revisited with new eyes 本地公路旅行——重訪熟悉的地方，用新的眼光
  • Book club — the same people, every month, a constant 讀書會——同一群人，每個月，穩定的存在
  • Volunteering — still giving, still connected to something larger 志工服務——仍在給予，仍與更大的事物相連
  • Growing herbs and vegetables for the table — small, productive, grounding 種植香草與蔬菜自用——小而有產出，讓人踏實
Money · 財務
Annual Budget · Bay Area
Reduced travel spend. More modest daily pace. But healthcare costs begin rising meaningfully in this decade — budget for that reality, not the optimistic version.
$60K per year × 10 years = $600,000
🪑 Phase
Three
Age 80–End · 第三階段
The No-Go Years
80 onward · 10 years estimated · 估計十年

The name sounds bleak. The reality is more nuanced. The No-Go years are not necessarily years of suffering — they are years of smaller radius. The world contracts, not because it has become less interesting, but because the body sensibly begins to negotiate. You work with what you have. You find depth in proximity rather than distance.

This is also the phase where the unexpected expenses live — healthcare, care support, home modifications, and ideally the AI-assisted technology that by the 2030s and 2040s will likely look very different from anything we can fully imagine today. A robot that helps around the home, powered by agents that genuinely understand context, is not science fiction for this generation. It is a reasonable budget line item.

Activities · 活動
  • A few road trips — closer to home, well-planned, still worth doing 幾次公路旅行——離家近一些，規劃周全，仍然值得
  • An annual cruise with friends — structured, comfortable, sociable 每年一次郵輪旅行——有組織、舒適、有社交
  • Book club — now a lifeline as much as a pleasure 讀書會——此時既是樂趣，也是生命線
  • Home as sanctuary — garden, kitchen, the quiet pleasures of a well-kept life 家作為庇護所——花園、廚房、井然有序的安靜生活
  • AI-assisted home support — by then, not a luxury but a practical tool AI 輔助居家支援——到那時，不是奢侈，而是實用工具

Nearly three million dollars. Said plainly, that number is alarming. Said in context, it is simply what a long life in an expensive place costs — spread over 35 years, adjusted for the fact that the Slow-Go years cost significantly less than the Go-Go years, and that the No-Go years, while more expensive in care costs, require far less of everything else. The number is large. It is also, with enough lead time and reasonable investment returns, achievable. The math is not the enemy. Waiting too long to look at it is.

將近三百萬美元。直說的話，這個數字很嚇人。
放在背景下看，它只是一段漫長的生命在昂貴地方的代價——
分散在三十五年裡，考慮到各階段的不同需求。
這個數字很大。但只要有足夠的時間和合理的投資回報，它是可以實現的。
數字不是敵人。太晚才去面對它，才是。

One more thing worth saying. Plans don't survive contact with life fully intact — health changes, markets move, priorities shift, people we love need things we didn't budget for. The point of the plan is not precision. It is orientation. Knowing roughly where you're going lets you notice when you've drifted, and course-correct before the drift becomes a problem. The big picture is not naive. The big picture is the only place where the whole thing makes sense.

The best financial plan is not the most optimized one. It is the one you can actually live with — through the Go-Go years when you are spending freely and the Slow-Go years when you are spending wisely and the No-Go years when you are spending on things you never thought you'd need to spend on.

Save enough to fund all three phases. Spend freely in the first. Spend thoughtfully in the second. Spend without guilt in the third. That is the whole plan. Everything else is detail.

最好的財務計劃不是最優化的那個。
而是你真正能與之共存的那個——
在大步走的年份自由地花，在慢慢走的年份謹慎地花，
在停下來的年份毫無愧疚地花。
存夠三個階段的錢。其他的，都是細節。
